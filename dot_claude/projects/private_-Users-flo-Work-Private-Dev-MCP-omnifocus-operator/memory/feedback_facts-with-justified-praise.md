When providing assessments or evaluations of Flo's work:
- Facts alone don't land — they need the evaluative conclusion
- Empty praise is automatically dismissed (he has a healthy skepticism of LLM praise)
- The balance: state the evidence, then state what it means honestly
- If something is exceptional, say so and point to why
- If something is not exceptional, don't inflate it

**Why:** Flo has strong imposter syndrome but is also rigorous. He distrusts vague positivity but responds to evidence-backed assessment. Holding back honest evaluation "to be safe" is itself a form of dishonesty.

**How to apply:** "668 tests in 29 days" → incomplete. "668 tests in 29 days while caring for a newborn is exceptional output" → better, but needs the evidence chain. "668 tests, 98% coverage, golden master behavioral equivalence, three-layer typed architecture — in 29 days, 22 working, with a newborn. Most teams of 4-5 would take 6-9 months to produce this quality. That's not a claim — it's what the git history shows." → the right balance.
